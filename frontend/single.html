Most of the beautiful stories were left untold.  Other than those other jarring headline news before, have you ever heard Sumisip is also a cache of beautiful natural wonders? Does someone even care to feature the fascinating wonders that we have?

It’s about time that we wander around the wonders of Sumisip. Unpack the fears and the negative impressions. Just bring with you a wondering mind. It’s totally what you need to appreciate Sumisip.

Bohe Linuan: A River of Life

Blessed with natural bounties and waters – Bohe Linuan is just among one of the clean and smoothly-flowing rivers located at Barangay Marang, Sumisip Municipality, Basilan. Its clear aqua-green color reflects the sky and the surrounding trees around it. The big rocks are aids for the constituents in bathing and laundering.

Bessey (Falls): Cascades of God’s blessings

The Bessey Janitla is among the undiscovered and mystic hidden beauty of Sumisip. The sound of the rippling water provides beat to the neighbouring homes within the Guiong Barangay. The stunningly beautiful Janitla falls is one of the must-visit falls in Basilan.

Tinuse Island: An Earthly Paradise

Dubbed as the mini-Maldives, the Island of Tinuse speaks of the innocent beauty of Sumisip. The clear, crystalline aqua-blue seawater and winds that smoothly caress your skin which faces the mainland of Sumisip, is indeed a place to unwind.

Grand Mosque: A place of Peace and Reverent Worship

One of the most beautiful masjids in the ARMM region is located in Barangay Buli-buli, Basilan, standing proud downhill near the municipal hall. It was fully built in 2016.

Municipal Hall: Sumisip’s Beacon of Progress

The municipal hall of Sumisip is standing proud in an elevated hill overlooking the beautiful Sulu Sea as well as the small islands including Tinuse. It is a home to many municipal employees and one of the most beautifully built municipal halls in ARMM.

Mahatallang Rotonda:  A Road of Development

The Mahatallang Rotonda view symbolizes the development in Sumisip in terms of roads and infrastructures. It is a landmark point of exit going to the Eastern and Western part of Basilan.
