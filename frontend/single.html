Growing up in Sumisip

Born into a family of political leaders and growing up in a municipality where peace is constantly challenged and sustained by enmity and distrust among people, the Mayor of Sumisip Municipality, Jul-Adnan P. Hataman, learned to turn the sad and painful reality he faced into dreams and promises of what he describes as seemingly impossible wonderful future for his beloved hometown.

Mayor Adnan, 31, spent most of his childhood in their humble abode in Barangay Buli-buli, Sumisip, Basilan where he stayed with his family and recalls most of his unforgettable childhood with his grandparents where he enjoyed a simple provincial life. For Adnan, it is an advantage for him that he was born to a family of leaders because at an early age, he was exposed to serving people.

Aside from his early exposure, his experiences inspired him to dream bigger for Sumisip. The glimpse of sadness that came across through the mayor’s eyes upon recalling how much he fears the impossibility to achieve peace in Sumisip was no concealable. What really inspired him the most to boldly take the challenge to run for politics was he wants his children to enjoy the beauty of Sumisip. He wanted Sumisip to be a destination goal for everyone just like how he becomes excited whenever his uncles bring him to another place. He didn’t want Sumisip to be a place of abandon or a municipality where no one would dare to visit just like how some would cringe at the idea of going to his comfortable town.

Becoming a Leader

In short, growing up in Sumisip ignited his young mind and heart to dream big. He was excited to see the change he wanted to see. When his father became the mayor, he had high hopes that finally somebody whom he knows would courageously walk the peace in Sumisip. Being his father’s hand, Adnan learned that becoming a leader is not as easy as icing a cake like what he expected. The conflict he thought would vanish in a clap, the development he thought would come like a magic required a lot of hard work, commitment, sweat and blood, and there were tough times that would make you choose between yourself and your people.

But that did not turn down the young Adnan to even dream bigger, instead, he wanted to follow his father’s steps although he knew that he will lead differently from that of his father’s. So in 2016, he found an opportunity to build the dream he had in his heart and mind for so many years. On his father’s last term, he was elected as the vice mayor. Their terms had the best of the father-and-son tandem–his father focused on restoring the peace which he designed himself through negotiations while Adnan centered his term on developing the infrastructures, education, sports, culture and providing the basic services that the people needed through walking projects to the regional government and the national government.

Realizing Dreams as Vice Mayor

As a vice mayor, he is seen every Thursday holding a gavel and presiding the sessions of the Sangguniang Bayan with the Councilors of the Sumisip Municipality. But Adnan was determined enough to do more to see realize the dreams he has for Sumisip. Sitting as the vice mayor empowered him more to finally turn his dreams into concrete reality thus he started with the road and infrastructure constantly bargaining for the full concretization the roads in Sumisip, establishment of new educational buildings, among others, with the regional government or Autonomous Region in Muslim  Mindanao so that transporting goods and services becomes easy.

Second, he focused on providing the basic services especially to distant areas that have no means of transportation or those places inaccessible to public services due to security instability. Third, he mobilized the youth and other sectoral organizations to help him in his advocacies. Fourth, he built inter-government network and partnered with non-government agencies to help him materialize the activities he wanted Sumisip to experience.

His Greatest Achievements as Vice Mayor

In his term serving as the vice mayor of the municipality, he considers Kalasig-lasigan Festival, the anniversary celebration of Sumisip Municipality every 8th day of April and is usually celebrated for a week-long series of events, as his greatest achievement. Kalasig-lasigan festival started in 2017 when he sketched it out from his experiences having been attended in ither cities. He admitted that at first, he was hesitant if Sumisip, then rising municipality, could handle such grand event for the first time. But the former Regional Governor Mujiv Hataman pushed him and gave him courage and assurance that he will support in any way.

The Kalasig-lasigan festival for three consecutive years marked many firsts of Sumisip and was the talk of the town for its annual success. The Municipality received many praises and good comments from the people and from those who witnessed the week-long celebration.

The Fruit of the Hard Work

Apart from the Kalasig-lasigan, one of the greatest milestones for Sumisip in his term as vice mayor, with his father being the mayor, is being awarded the Seal of Local Good Governance twice in a row in 2017 and 2018 by the Department of the Interior and Local Government.

The award is a significant validation that indeed, his dreams for Sumisip are gradually achieved not just in one aspect, more so, in financial administration, disaster preparedness, social protection, peace and order, business friendliness and competitiveness, environmental protection and tourism, culture and the arts. The collective efforts bore fruits.

Indeed, a Dreamer in Deed

For the young mayor, everything was just a dream until he boldly took step putting his plans into acts. In the pursuit of his dreams which are not just for himself but also for his family and for Sumisip as a whole, he was able to do things he thought was impossible before. His leadership story is something we all could relate to, for once you believe in yourself that you can, eventually you will find yourself achieving it.

Truly, his term as the vice mayor was not enough to build his dreams completely. Three years went fast and yet the youthful enthusiasm of the municipality remains in hype when he run and won again in the 2019 elections. As he started his legacy in 2016, the constituents of Sumisip are looking forward to his programs and projects as a mayor. Although he is starting with the internal management of the employees and officers to fully capacitate the workforce for his first one hundred days, ha has set his platforms in education, livelihood, health and public services, peace and tourism.

Mayor Jul Adnan P. Hataman is indeed a dreamer in deed. He is one who does not just dream but also builds the dream in reality.
